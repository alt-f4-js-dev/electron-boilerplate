Explanatory Notes

Form BPD_BS01

Form BPD_BS01 must be completed and submitted together with the application for approval of building plans for projects subjected to buildable design requirement.

For projects with multiple blocks, please submit 1 copy of the BPD_BS01 form for every block.

For additional building blocks, select the SUMMARY sheet. Click the "Add Block" button. Click OK to confirm to add a block.

To delete a building block, click the "Delete Block" button. Enter the sheet name of the block to delete. Click OK to confirm deletion.

Structural System

(1) At least 80% of the steel reinforcement for composite slab must be welded mesh.

(2) The use of welded mesh is mandated for all developments where cast in-situ slab has been adopted in the design. The minimum usage of welded mesh must be at least 65% of all cast in-situ slab area.

(3) For designs of residential non-landed projects that incorporate household shelters, it is mandatory for these household shelters to be precast.

(4) High strength concrete of at least Grade 70 must be used for at least 5% of the total concrete volume.

(5) Self-compacting concrete must be used for at least 30% of the total concrete volume.

(6) Diaphragm wall must be adopted for at least 65% of the permanent retaining wall at basement.

Wall System

(7) Drywall partition consists of prefabricated boards typically made of gypsum (or other materials such as calcium silicate or fibre-based cement) that are screw-fastened to both sides of a metal stud framing system. No wet trades are involved. The type and number of layers of the boards, and the insulation material within the space formed by the metal framing system are determined by the desired strength, acoustic, fire and other performance criteria required of the partition. For mandatory use of drywall under item 1.1, the drywall used must achieve a performance grading of Severe Duty for strength and robustness.

(8) Lightweight concrete panels include autoclaved lightweight concrete (ALC) panels, autoclaved aerated concrete (AAC) panels.

(9) Precision blocks refer to lightweight concrete blocks that have precise dimensions (± 1mm dimensional tolerance) and can be laid on thin bed adhesive mortar.

(10) The use of brickwall/blockwall, once used, must be indicated and shown under wall system by its wall length. Demerit points for using brickwall/blockwall will be auto-generated under item 14.5 on Demerit Points.
