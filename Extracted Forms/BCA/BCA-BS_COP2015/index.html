Structural System

(1) At least 80% of the steel reinforcement for composite slab must be welded mesh.


(2) The use of welded mesh is mandated for all developments where cast in-situ slab has been adopted in the design. The minimum usage of welded mesh must be at least 65% of all cast in-situ slab area.


(3) Precast Joints are to be taken from the following guides.

(i) BCA's Buildable Solutions for High-Rise Residential Development
(ii) BCA's Structural Precast Concrete Handbook
(iii) BCA's Buildable Solutions for Landed Residential Development in Singapore
(iv) HDB's Precast Pictorial Guide

(4) At least 65% of the precast joint types used in a project must come from any of the publications mentioned in (3).


(5) At least 65% of the precast component must use mechanical connection.


(6) Innovative structural steel connections refer to those that do not require site welding and allow faster erection and easy on-site installation of steel members.


(7) High strength concrete of at least Grade 70 must be used for at least 5% of the total concrete volume.


(8) Self-compacting concrete must be used for at least 30% of the total concrete volume.


(9) Diaphragm wall must be adopted for at least 65% of the permanent retaining wall at basement.


Wall System

(1) Lightweight concrete panels include autoclaved lightweight concrete (ALC) panels, autoclaved aerated concrete (AAC) panels.

(2) Precision blocks refer to lightweight concrete blocks that have precise dimensions (± 1mm dimensional tolerance) and can be laid on thin bed adhesive mortar.

(3) Drywall partition consists of prefabricated boards typically made of gypsum (or other materials such as calcium silicate or fibre-based cement) that are screw-fastened to both sides of a metal stud framing system. No wet trades are involved. The type and number of layers of the boards, and the insulation material within the space formed by the metal framing system are determined by the desired strength, acoustic, fire and other performance criteria required of the partition. For mandatory use of drywall under item 1.1, the drywall used must achieve a performance grading of Severe Duty for strength and robustness.

(4) The use of brickwall/blockwall, once used, must be indicated and shown under wall system by its wall length. Demerit points for using brickwall/blockwall will be auto-generated under item B.5 on Demerit Points.
